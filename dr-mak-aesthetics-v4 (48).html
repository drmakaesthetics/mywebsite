Performed by a GMC-registered doctor in a clinical setting. All procedures are subject to clinical assessment and carried out where appropriate. Individual results vary.

Skin Lesion & Surface Procedures

Minor skin lesions are extremely common and most are entirely benign. Dr MAK can assess, diagnose and where appropriate treat a range of surface skin lesions using established clinical techniques. The following images show examples of conditions that patients may wish to discuss.

Skin Tags
Benign Moles
Seborrhoeic Keratoses
Warts & Verrucae
Shave Excision

Removal of raised skin lesions such as skin tags and benign elevated moles using a shave technique. Quick, performed under local anaesthetic.

Curettage

Scraping technique used to remove superficial skin lesions including seborrhoeic keratoses and warts. Minimal scarring with appropriate aftercare.

Cautery

Thermal cautery to treat and seal small skin lesions, skin tags and superficial vascular lesions. Often combined with curettage for best results.

Punch Biopsy

Diagnostic skin biopsy using a circular punch instrument. Used where histological confirmation of a lesion is required prior to further treatment.

Cryotherapy

Liquid nitrogen treatment to freeze and destroy benign lesions such as warts, verrucae and seborrhoeic keratoses. Non-surgical with minimal downtime.

Epidermoid & Sebaceous Cyst Assessment

Clinical assessment of cysts with removal where appropriate. Procedure involves incision, expression of contents and removal of the cyst wall under local anaesthetic.

Lipoma Assessment & Removal

Assessment of benign fatty lumps with removal of suitable lipomas under local anaesthetic. Larger or deeper lipomas may require onward referral.

Peri-Articular & Joint Injections

Peri-articular and joint injections are targeted treatments used to reduce inflammation, relieve pain, and improve mobility in affected joints such as the shoulder and knee. These injections are typically used for conditions such as inflammation, tendon-related pain, or joint irritation. All injections are subject to clinical assessment and offered where appropriate.

Shoulder Injections

Targeted injections to the shoulder joint or surrounding structures. Used for conditions including shoulder impingement, rotator cuff tendinopathy and bursitis where appropriate.

Knee Injections

Intra-articular or peri-articular knee injections for conditions such as osteoarthritic knee pain, patellar tendinopathy or localised joint inflammation where clinically indicated.

Other Peri-Articular Injections

Injections to other joints and surrounding soft tissue structures including tendons and bursae. Discussed and assessed on an individual basis prior to treatment.
